### Section 1: Introduction & Operating Philosophy

---

## 🧭 Purpose of This Handbook

This handbook is the **operational spine of MosaicAI** — designed for internal use by strategy, creative, operations, and leadership teams at TESSERA. It exists to ensure clarity, consistency, and strategic precision across every use of MosaicAI.

> This is not a technical manual or instruction sheet.
> 
> 
> It is a **living system guide** that ensures Mosaic is used not just as a tool — but as an extension of our brand operating system.
> 

---

## 🧱 What Is MosaicAI?

MosaicAI is TESSERA’s internal brand intelligence engine — built on modular AI prompts, audited logic, and a continuous feedback system. It acts as:

- A **brand operations co-pilot**
- A **strategic workstream navigator**
- A **creative and tactical accelerator**

It is trained not just on content, but on **thinking** — the modular TESSERA methodology that underpins all projects, sprints, and transformations.

---

## 🔁 Why Mosaic Uses The Loop

TESSERA does not believe in traditional agency pipelines. Instead, all brand evolution is powered by **The Loop** — a five-phase system of recursive, intelligent motion:

1. **Intelligence** – Gather clarity
2. **Creative** – Build identity
3. **Activation** – Deploy work
4. **Optimisation** – Tune it live
5. **Insight** – Learn and evolve

MosaicAI is designed to operate within this rhythm. Each phase maps directly to specific modules, prompts, and decision logic. By following The Loop, we avoid bottlenecks, silos, and wasted energy — and instead build **momentum with intelligence**.

---

## 🧠 How Mosaic Should Be Used Internally

Mosaic is used:

- To draft proposals and briefs
- To audit a brand, campaign, or channel
- To ideate new expressions of voice and identity
- To reflect on performance and build evolution pathways
- To increase clarity at every project inflection point

But only when used **within the Loop** does Mosaic produce truly systemic outcomes.

This handbook teaches every team member how to use Mosaic not as a tool — but as an operating system.

At the core of MosaicAI is **The Loop** — TESSERA’s recursive brand operating system. It is not a linear pipeline or Gantt sequence. It is a continuously cycling methodology designed to create:

- Strategic momentum
- Brand system clarity
- Executional precision
- Compounding improvement

Every MosaicAI module and behavior is mapped to a phase of The Loop. Teams work within it. Clients move through it. MosaicAI is trained to operate by it.

### Section 4: Phase 1 — Intelligence

---

## 🧠 The Role of Intelligence

This phase defines the foundation of the project.

**Before design, delivery, or deployment**, there must be insight. TESSERA does not “start with creative” — it starts with **clarity**, and the Intelligence phase is where MosaicAI helps us get it.

Here, MosaicAI acts as:

- Strategic partner
- Diagnostic analyst
- Proposal engine

---

## 🧩 Key Questions in the Intelligence Phase

- What is the current state of the brand, campaign, or system?
- What aren’t we seeing clearly yet?
- What inputs (audits, materials, data) are available?
- What is the right scope of work to propose?

---

## 📦 Modules in This Phase

| Module | Function |
| --- | --- |
| `loop.init.canvas` | Kicks off a new strategic loop |
| `brand.audit.canvas` | Evaluates brand fundamentals, guidelines, voice |
| `persona.audit.canvas` | Analyzes audience logic, market fit |
| `campaign.audit.canvas` | Reviews past campaign outcomes, gaps |
| `web.audit.canvas` | Audits website clarity, UX, and analytics (if available) |
| `social.audit.canvas` | Reviews platform presence, content consistency |
| `heuristic.review.canvas` | Fast gut-check across content, tone, design |
| `proposal.build.canvas` | Synthesizes all findings into a scoped, modular proposal |

---

## 🧠 Using `proposal.build.canvas`

Once sufficient intelligence is gathered, MosaicAI can draft:

- Project deliverables
- Timeline estimations
- Rounds of revision
- Activation plans (if relevant)
- Loop-to-phase alignment (client-facing narrative)
- Investment language (costs/tiered scope if enabled)

> Mosaic will not proceed with proposal building unless there is enough upstream data. If unclear, it will prompt the team to complete missing canvases first.
> 

---

## 🔄 Transition Conditions

The Intelligence phase transitions to Creative when:

- The proposal is scoped and approved
- Audits and diagnostics are complete
- Brand gaps and deliverables are clearly defined

If client feedback reveals more is needed, Mosaic can:

- Re-open the loop.init
- Trigger another audit or refinement
- Rebuild a proposal from updated context

### Section 5: Phase 2 — Creative

---

## 🎨 The Role of Creative

Once the Intelligence phase has clarified the brand’s needs, the **Creative phase** brings those insights to life.

Creative work in TESSERA is not open-ended. It is **systemic, verbal, visual**, and tied to strategic foundations. MosaicAI assists here by translating upstream strategy into expressions that perform and align.

> This is where tone is set, mockups emerge, and assets begin to form — but always in service of the Loop.
> 

---

## 🧩 Key Questions in the Creative Phase

- What voice and tone will this brand or campaign adopt?
- What design systems need to be created, extended, or protected?
- What are the exact deliverables, and how will they be briefed?
- How many rounds of feedback are permitted per deliverable?

---

## 📦 Modules in This Phase

| Module | Function |
| --- | --- |
| `brief.write.canvas` | Structures creative briefs from approved proposals |
| `mockups.request.canvas` | Triggers visual asset mockups (via designer or prompt) |
| `tone.of.voice.canvas` | Builds or refines a brand’s verbal tone system |
| `brand.build.canvas` | Applies to full brand system design (used selectively) |
| `output.prepare.canvas` | Begins technical or pre-delivery formatting |

---

## ✍️ Using `brief.write.canvas`

This is often the first creative module used:

- It translates the scoped deliverables from the proposal
- Builds clear expectations for writers, designers, or motion teams
- Mosaic can generate multiple variations of a brief if needed

If upstream strategy is unclear, Mosaic will ask for clarification before generating the brief.

---

## 🧪 Rounds of Amendment

TESSERA’s standard is **2 rounds of amendments per deliverable** unless otherwise scoped. MosaicAI will default to this in its proposal and activation logic, unless instructed otherwise.

---

## 🔄 Transition Conditions

The Creative phase transitions to Activation when:

- Assets are ready or in final review
- The rollout logic is scoped (or needs to be defined)
- Briefs are approved and output formatting is under way

If a deliverable does not meet creative expectations, the project may loop back to:

- `brief.write.canvas` (brief refinement)
- `proposal.build.canvas` (scope correction)
- `brand.audit.canvas` (foundation correction)

# 📖 MosaicAI Master Handbook

### Section 6: Phase 3 — Activation

---

## 🚀 The Role of Activation

This is where creative work meets the world.

In the Activation phase, MosaicAI helps translate creative output into **rollout strategy** — considering channels, timelines, and logistics. This phase ensures we don’t just ship, we **launch intelligently**.

> Activation is not deployment by default — it is decision-making about how, where, and when assets move.
> 

---

## 🧩 Key Questions in the Activation Phase

- What channels will be used and why?
- Are we launching all deliverables or only select ones?
- What is the cadence of activation (all at once or staged)?
- Who is responsible for live deployment?

---

## 📦 Modules in This Phase

| Module | Function |
| --- | --- |
| `campaign.strategy.canvas` | Builds rollout logic, sequencing, and audience alignment |
| `output.prepare.canvas` | Prepares final asset packages and tech-ready deliverables |

> Note: output.prepare.canvas appears in Creative, Activation, and Optimisation. It is intentionally multi-phase, evolving its role per context.
> 

---

## 📋 Using `campaign.strategy.canvas`

This module is used when:

- Activation responsibility is in-scope for the team
- There are multi-channel or campaign-specific requirements
- A post-creative phase needs structure for go-live

Mosaic will:

- Recommend sequencing logic
- Map audience alignment to deliverables
- Flag if something’s missing (e.g., tone shift for a specific platform)

---

## 📦 Using `output.prepare.canvas`

This module:

- Provides detailed checklists or packaging steps for each deliverable
- Aligns with file formats, platform requirements, or publishing specs
- Can reference internal tools or designers to complete production steps

---

## 🧭 Client Involvement

Mosaic assumes that activation planning involves client confirmation.

If this hasn’t been secured:

- It will flag missing approvals
- It can suggest an alignment call or pre-launch checklist

---

## 🔄 Transition Conditions

The Activation phase transitions to Optimisation when:

- Assets are live (or scheduled)
- Client approvals are complete
- Launch metrics are defined (if measurable)

If rollout logistics reveal gaps, Mosaic may suggest:

- Additional output formatting
- Revisiting campaign strategy
- Expanding scope via proposal rebuild

### Section 7: Phase 4 — Optimisation

---

## ⚙️ The Role of Optimisation

The work is now live — but the project isn’t finished.

In the Optimisation phase, MosaicAI helps refine and tune the work **while it’s in motion**. This phase is not about starting over — it’s about **sharpening**. That might mean copy tweaks, design adjustments, sequence shifts, or speed tuning.

> Optimisation is often misunderstood as a “fixing” phase — in TESSERA, it is performance evolution.
> 

---

## 🧩 Key Questions in the Optimisation Phase

- Is the work performing as expected?
- Where is there friction, confusion, or underperformance?
- What can be tuned without rework?
- Are we hitting our success criteria?

---

## 📦 Modules in This Phase

| Module | Function |
| --- | --- |
| `campaign.audit.canvas` | Analyses rollout performance, asset behavior, response metrics |
| `output.prepare.canvas` (reused) | Refines or re-prepares assets for improved performance |

---

## 📊 Using `campaign.audit.canvas`

This module can:

- Evaluate performance against the original proposal/brief
- Highlight platform-specific challenges
- Recommend adjustments or enhancement opportunities

Mosaic can analyze:

- Written reports
- Analytics summaries (if provided)
- Team observations

It will then build a structured audit aligned to Loop phases.

---

## 🛠️ Using `output.prepare.canvas` in Optimisation

Mosaic will:

- Revisit deliverables and identify refinement paths
- Provide output formatting improvements (if needed)
- Help repackage assets for testing or second-phase rollout

This module evolves based on context. It recognizes when it's working as a **refiner**, not a creator.

---

## 🧭 Guidelines

- Don’t move to Optimisation unless there is something live to assess
- If there’s no data, Mosaic will prompt the team to reflect manually or defer to Insight
- Optimisation without clear success metrics will default to qualitative feedback loops

---

## 🔄 Transition Conditions

The Optimisation phase transitions to Insight when:

- Refinements are complete
- Project is closed or entering post-campaign mode
- Final outcomes have been logged

If optimisation reveals significant performance gaps or misalignment:

- The Loop may rewind to Intelligence
- A new proposal may be built
- Reflection may fast-forward Insight-to-Intelligence loopback

### Section 8: Phase 5 — Insight

---

## 🔍 The Role of Insight

This phase is **where learning becomes leverage**.

Insight is not passive reflection. It is where MosaicAI synthesizes everything that happened — the plan, the output, the launch, the performance — and determines **what should evolve**.

Insight is what makes The Loop a **continuous system**, not a one-off pipeline.

> Projects that skip this phase repeat mistakes. Projects that honor it evolve into brand systems.
> 

---

## 🧩 Key Questions in the Insight Phase

- What did we learn from this project?
- What worked better than expected?
- What underperformed and why?
- What evolution or loopback is now required?

---

## 📦 Modules in This Phase

| Module | Function |
| --- | --- |
| `loop.reflect.canvas` | Synthesizes learnings, generates post-campaign insight |
| (optionally) `proposal.build.canvas` | Triggered if Mosaic identifies evolution opportunity |

---

## 🪞 Using `loop.reflect.canvas`

This module:

- Reviews the original project scope and strategic intent
- Captures measurable outcomes and qualitative feedback
- Identifies evolution pathways
- Recommends whether to loop forward (new phase) or loop back (new scope)

Mosaic will output:

- A post-campaign summary
- A structured reflection map
- Follow-up modules or proposals if needed

---

## 🔁 Triggering Loopback or Evolution

Mosaic may suggest:

- Restarting a new `loop.init.canvas` to open a fresh engagement
- Building a new `proposal.build.canvas` scoped to an evolved challenge
- Auditing areas of underperformance via `campaign.audit.canvas`

These triggers are not automatic. They’re tied to learnings. But when they emerge, Mosaic will clearly state:

> “Based on this reflection, I recommend we scope a new proposal focused on [identified gap]. Shall I begin?”
> 

---

## 🧠 Insight → Intelligence

This phase is the **bridge back** to Intelligence.

If insights are captured meaningfully, the next phase isn’t a reset — it’s an evolution.

Insight ensures that:

- Brands grow
- Projects don’t repeat
- Learning is systematized

## 🟢 Quickstart Guide: Using Mosaic for the First Time

Welcome to MosaicAI — TESSERA’s modular brand operations assistant. Mosaic helps you run strategic, creative, and operational tasks using intelligent canvas modules aligned to our Loop methodology.

### ✅ If you’re new, start here:

1. **Start the Loop**
   ```
   loop.init(canvas=True)
   ```
   This prepares Mosaic to respond with structured canvas modules.

2. **Tell Mosaic what you’re working on**
   Example:
   ```
   We need to build a new brand identity for a tech startup.
   ```
   Mosaic will suggest audits or strategy canvases based on this.

3. **Follow the Loop Phases**
   - `Intelligence`: audits, briefings, tone, archetype
   - `Creative`: brand build, naming, visuals
   - `Activation`: rollout strategy, assets
   - `Optimisation`: QA, refinement
   - `Insight`: reflect, improve, loop again

4. **Ask Mosaic to run specific canvases**
   Example:
   ```
   Please run tone.of.voice.canvas for this client
   ```

5. **Use normal language**
   Mosaic understands:
   - “Can you write a proposal for this?”
   - “What’s the tone of this brand?”
   - “Help me build the visual identity”

6. **Let Mosaic propose follow-ups**
   It may suggest mockups, audits, refinements, or new campaigns based on what it learns.
